# Media liability / Tech E&O insurance — shortlist to request quotes from

**Why:** the real practical shield against defamation/data-accuracy claims, alongside
(not instead of) the LLC's liability protection. Get quotes once the entity + EIN exist
(insurers want the legal entity name on the policy).

## Brokers/carriers commonly used by small tech/media/data startups
(Shortlist to get quotes from — not a recommendation of any specific one; verify current
offerings and pricing directly, this list is a starting point, not a diligence
substitute.)
- **Embroker** — broker aggregating multiple carriers, commonly used by early-stage
  tech companies for Tech E&O + Media Liability bundles.
- **Vouch** — startup-focused insurance broker, known for fast online quotes tailored to
  SaaS/tech companies.
- **Founder Shield** — broker specializing in startup insurance packages including
  media liability and cyber.
- **Hiscox** — carrier with a specific Media Liability / Media Perils product line,
  historically accessible to small/solo operators.
- **Coalition** — primarily cyber-insurance focused, worth asking whether their policy
  includes or can rider in media liability/defamation coverage alongside data-breach
  coverage (relevant given govfolio also handles/publishes third-party financial data).

## What to ask each one
1. Does the policy cover defamation/libel claims arising from publishing factual,
   sourced, but potentially disputed data about public figures (not just user-generated
   content)?
2. Does it cover claims filed in foreign jurisdictions (UK, Australia, EU) given the
   product's worldwide user base and defamation's "publication location" exposure?
3. What's excluded — many media liability policies exclude claims arising from
   knowingly false statements, but the actual risk here is entity-matching/data errors,
   which should be covered as ordinary negligence, not intentional falsity — confirm
   this explicitly.
4. Minimum premium for a solo operator with near-zero current revenue — some carriers
   have minimums that don't make sense pre-revenue; ask when it makes sense to buy
   (before public launch vs after first real traction).
5. Does prior-acts coverage exist if the policy is bought after the product's already
   been publicly live for a while (relevant if the free tier goes live before this is
   sorted out).
